## Scenario C: The "Warm Network Extraction" Model

**Target:** £100,000
**Approach:** Monolithic push on existing network

Assumptions:
- 100 warm contacts in network
- 30% response rate = 30 conversations
- 20% meeting rate = 6 meetings
- 50% close rate = 3 closes

**To hit £100K:**
- Need 3 closes at £33,333 each (unlikely)
- More realistic: 4 closes averaging £25,000

## Scenario D: The "Hybrid" Model

**Target:** £100,000
**Approach:** Mix of premium offers + upsells

| Component | Price | Quantity | Revenue |
|-----------|-------|----------|---------|
| Premium Audit (lead) | £2,500 | 10 | £25,000 |
| Audit → Sprint upgrade | £12,500 | 4 | £50,000 |
| Sprint → Enterprise | £25,000 | 1 | £25,000 |

**Total:** £100,000

---

## Highest Probability Path: Scenario D

**Why:**
1. Starts with lower-commitment £2,500 audit (easier close)
2. Upgrades existing clients (warmer than cold outreach)
3. Enterprise adds-on increase ticket size
4. Multiple revenue streams from same contact

**Same-day execution:**
- Morning: Push 10 audit inquiries
- Mid-day: Convert 4 audits to sprints
- Afternoon: Close 1 enterprise upgrade
- Evening: Follow up on any remaining interest

---

## Decision: Primary Offer Stack

The system will be built around **Scenario D (Hybrid)** as the primary path:

1. **Entry Point:** Premium Audit (£2,500)
2. **Upgrade Path:** Audit → Sprint (£12,500 more)
3. **Upsell Path:** Sprint → Enterprise (£25,000 more)
4. **Fallback:** Team Workshop (£5,000)

This gives multiple same-day revenue vectors.
